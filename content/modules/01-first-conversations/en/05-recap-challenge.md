## Hook

A week ago, "AI" might have meant robots from movies. Now you've talked to one, put it to work on a real task, caught it being confidently wrong, and know exactly what never to paste. That's more than most adults using these tools every day. Let's lock it in.

## Core idea

One sentence per session — this is the whole module:

1. **Meet AI:** An AI chatbot is a well-read text predictor — not a search engine, not a brain, not a person.
2. **Your first real task:** Concrete beats vague — say what you want, give the details, describe what good looks like, then iterate.
3. **Confidently wrong:** AI hallucinates; the more an answer matters, the more you check it.
4. **Safety & privacy:** The billboard test — strip identities, keep the situation.

If you can explain these four to someone at dinner, you've got Module 1.

## Show me

Your "you can now..." checklist:

- ✅ Start a conversation with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini and hold a real back-and-forth.
- ✅ Write a prompt with the three parts: what you want, the details, what good looks like.
- ✅ Ask "are you sure?" and check important answers in another source.
- ✅ Rewrite any prompt to remove private info before sending.
- ✅ Find the privacy settings and delete-chat option in your AI tool.

Anything unchecked? That session is a 15-minute revisit away.

## Your turn

**The weekly challenge:** between now and the next family dinner, use AI for **one real thing** in your life — not an exercise, a real thing. Ideas if you're stuck:

- Plan something (a trip day, a birthday, a week of lunches).
- Learn something ("explain how mortgages work like I'm 12").
- Make something (a poem for someone, a workout plan, a packing list).
- Fix something (a tricky message you need to send, a schedule conflict).

Then **share it at dinner**: what you asked, what you got, and one thing that surprised you. Bonus points for sharing a failure — those teach the family more than the wins.

- **Mode A — Go do it:** This one happens in real life. Come back and mark complete after dinner.

## Wrap-up

You're no longer a beginner: you can converse, task, verify, and stay safe. Module 2 makes your prompts dramatically better with three patterns the pros use.
