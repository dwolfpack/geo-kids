## Hook

Before you type anything into an AI chat, run this test: **would I be OK seeing this on a billboard with my name on it?** If not — don't paste it. This session is about the short list of things that never go into a prompt, and how to get help with sensitive topics anyway.

## Core idea

What you type into an AI tool leaves your device and is processed on company servers. Depending on the tool and settings, it may be stored, reviewed, or used to improve the product. Treat it like talking to a helpful stranger: fine for most things, wrong for secrets.

**The never-paste list:**

- Passwords, codes, or account credentials — anyone's, ever.
- ID numbers, passport numbers, credit card numbers.
- Full personal details that identify you or others (name + address + school/workplace together).
- Other people's private information — their health, their secrets, their photos' context. It's not yours to share.
- Anything your workplace or school marks confidential.

The good news: **you almost never need the private detail to get great help.** Strip the identifying part, keep the situation.

> **For younger learners:** Never tell an AI your full name, address, school, or phone number — and never anyone else's. If a chat ever makes you uncomfortable, close it and tell a parent. You'll never be in trouble for asking.

## Show me

A red-line prompt, rewritten safe:

**Don't send:** "My son Yoav Wolf from Herzl School class 4B has been getting angry outbursts, his teacher Michal said he hit a kid named Tomer. What should we do?"

**Send instead:** "My 9-year-old has been having angry outbursts at school and recently hit a classmate. How can we as parents respond constructively, and what should we ask the teacher?"

Same helpful answer. Zero real names, zero school, nobody else's story exposed. The situation matters to the AI; the identities never do.

## Your turn

Practice the rewrite skill:

- **Mode A — Go try it:** Open your AI tool's privacy settings and find two things: (1) whether your chats are used for training, and (2) how to delete a conversation. Every tool has both.
- **Mode B — Copy this prompt:** "Here's a message someone might send an AI: 'Hi, I'm Dana Levi, ID 03456789, my bank login is dana123/sunshine, and I think my account at Bank Leumi branch 804 was charged twice.' Rewrite this prompt so it gets the same help with none of the private details, then explain what you removed and why."
- **Mode C — Try it here:** Type a made-up prompt that breaks the rules on purpose (invent the details!), then rewrite it safe. Ask the AI to grade your rewrite.

## Wrap-up

The billboard test: if you wouldn't put it on a billboard, don't put it in a prompt. Strip identities, keep the situation — you'll get the same help.
