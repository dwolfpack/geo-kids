## Hook

You've probably used AI today without noticing — your phone's autocomplete, your map's traffic prediction, your video feed's recommendations. But there's one kind of AI you talk to directly, and it can help you write, plan, learn, and think. That's the one we're meeting today.

## Core idea

An AI chatbot (like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini) is a very well-read assistant that writes its answers **one word at a time**, predicting what a good response should look like based on enormous amounts of text it learned from.

Here's what it is **not**:

- **Not a search engine.** It doesn't look things up in a database of facts (unless you ask it to search). It generates answers from patterns it learned.
- **Not a brain.** It doesn't "know" things the way you do, and it doesn't remember you between conversations unless the tool is built to.
- **Not a person.** It can sound warm, funny, even opinionated — but there's no one home. It's a very sophisticated text predictor.

One analogy and we're done: it's like a friend who has read the whole library but sometimes misremembers — brilliant to brainstorm with, worth double-checking on facts.

> **For younger learners:** Imagine a parrot that has heard every book ever written. It can say amazing, helpful things — but it's still repeating patterns, not thinking like you do.

## Show me

Here's a first prompt and what came back:

**Prompt:** "Hi! I'm new to AI chat. Introduce yourself in 3 sentences, and tell me one surprising thing you can help with."

**Response:** "Hi! I'm Claude, an AI assistant made by Anthropic — I can help with writing, planning, explaining things, and thinking through problems. I don't browse your files or remember past chats unless you share them. One surprising thing: I'm great at turning your messy thoughts into a clear list — try pasting in a jumble of ideas sometime."

Notice: it's friendly, it's clear about its limits, and it invites a task. That's a normal first conversation.

## Your turn

Pick your mode and say hello:

- **Mode A — Go try it:** Open [claude.ai](https://claude.ai), [chatgpt.com](https://chatgpt.com), or [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com). Type a greeting and ask it to introduce itself.
- **Mode B — Copy this prompt:** "Hi! I've never talked to an AI before. Introduce yourself in 3 sentences, then ask me one question to get to know what I might use you for."
- **Mode C — Try it here:** Use the chat box below. Say hi, ask it anything you're curious about, and ask it what it *can't* do.

Whatever mode you pick: ask at least one follow-up question. Conversations, not single questions, are where these tools shine.

## Wrap-up

An AI chatbot is a well-read text predictor — powerful, helpful, but not a search engine, a brain, or a person.

**Reflection:** What surprised you most about its answer — the content, or the way it talked?
